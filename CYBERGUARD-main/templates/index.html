Stay Safe Online

Practical steps tailored for Indian students and youth to protect yourself and others.

S

Do Not Respond to Bullies

Responding often escalates the situation. Block, report, and step back before things get worse.

E

Save Screenshots as Evidence

Screenshot messages, usernames, links, dates, and threats before blocking or deleting anything.

T

Tell a Trusted Adult

Talk to a parent, teacher, school counsellor, elder sibling, or class mentor. You do not have to handle it alone.

R

Report on Every Platform

Instagram, WhatsApp, Snapchat, YouTube, and gaming platforms have report tools. Use them early.

M

Protect Your Mental Health

Take breaks from social media, speak to someone you trust, and call a helpline if you feel overwhelmed.

P

Tighten Privacy Settings

Set posts to friends only, disable public tagging, review who can message you, and avoid sharing personal details.

Understanding Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying can happen through WhatsApp, Instagram, Snapchat, gaming chats, school groups, and public comment sections.

H

Harassment

Repeated targeted attacks, unwanted messages, stalking, public shaming, or pressure campaigns.

H

Hate Speech

Content attacking someone based on caste, religion, gender, language, region, identity, or community.

T

Threats

Direct or implied intent to cause physical harm, emotional harm, humiliation, or fear.

I

Insults

Name-calling, demeaning language, mockery, or personal attacks designed to shame someone.

E

Exclusion

Deliberately leaving someone out of groups, chats, teams, or online communities to isolate them.
